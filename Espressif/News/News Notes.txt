China’s Espressif Finds Low-Cost Wi-Fi Chip Niche
Russ Arensman
17 June 2016

- Two years ago, Shanghai-based Espressif Systems was a struggling Chinese fabless semiconductor company best known for designing chips for other clients.

- Espressif’s fortunes improved significantly however, after the August, 2014 launch of its ESP8266 SOC. They have sold millions of these low-cost wi-fi networking chips, while attracting a devoted following among hobbyists and hackers

- Meantime, Espressif’s workforce has expanded from 20 to about 100, and it's engineers are putting the finishing touches on another, more powerful, wireless SOC – the ESP32

- The ESP8266 is essentially a low-power mcu that allows users to add 802.11BGN to a wide array of electronic devices

	*has won praise for its performance and easy programming
	*What really caught attention was price, as low as $1 each in moderate quantities
	
- But what really caught users’ attention was the chip’s price-- as low as $1 each in moderate quantities. That has allowed circuit board makers to build and sell ready-to-use ESP8266-based wi-fi modules for as little as $5 – far less than the $10, $20 and even higher prices of modules from competitors such as Broadcom, Particle, Qualcomm and Texas Instruments
	*Key - $5 vs. $10-$20
	*“Nothing else comes even close to this pricing”
	
- "The ESP8266 has gotten quite a big following in the Arduino world,” Goins said. “It doesn’t take an EE degree anymore to do this stuff"

- 20M 8266's sold in 2014, 50M quotes for 2015

- worldwide wireless networking chip market, which IHS Research estimates was worth $7.7 billion in 2015

- Even if only wi-fi enabled IoT devices are considered, and Espressif sold 50 million chips to just that market, its shipments would have been less than 2 percent of the 3.04 billion IoT wi-fi chips that IHS says were shipped in 2015
	*2% of market

- Espressif is currently successful primarily in Chinese markets, low current penetration in US markets

- the ESP32 offers faster Wi-Fi, two varieties of Bluetooth, more memory and hundreds of other improvements
